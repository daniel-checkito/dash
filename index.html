I've watched AI automation projects die the same death 6 times. Week 1: excitement. Week 2: real progress. Week 3: everything breaks. It's never the technology. It's always the same 3 things nobody covers in the tutorial videos: 1/ No human in the loop for edge cases The automation works perfectly — until it doesn't. And when it fails with no oversight, it fails loudly. 2/ The prompt worked in testing, not production Real data is messier than your test data. Always. 3/ Nobody owns the maintenance "Set and forget" is a myth. Automations need tending. The fix is simpler than you think: Build in a weekly 15-min review. Keep a human approval step for anything customer-facing. Document your edge cases as you find them. Automation isn't a destination. It's a practice.
